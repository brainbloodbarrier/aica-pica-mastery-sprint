# AICA & PICA: Quick Reference Guide
## Microsurgical Anatomy of the Cerebellar Arteries

**Based on**: Rhoton's Posterior Cranial Fossa Microsurgical Anatomy

---

## Posterior Circulation Overview

### The Three Cerebellar Arteries

| Artery | Origin | Primary Territory | Key Surgical Relationships |
|--------|--------|-------------------|---------------------------|
| **SCA** | Distal basilar (near PCA bifurcation) | Superior cerebellar surface, superior peduncle | CN III, CN IV |
| **AICA** | Mid-basilar artery | Anteroinferior cerebellum, middle peduncle, inner ear | CN VII, VIII; internal auditory canal |
| **PICA** | Vertebral artery | Posteroinferior cerebellum, inferior peduncle, lateral medulla | CN IX, X, XI, XII; fourth ventricle |

**Clinical Note**: AICA and PICA are encountered in most posterior fossa surgeries including CPA tumors, fourth ventricle lesions, foramen magnum pathology, and vertebrobasilar aneurysms.

---

## AICA (Anteroinferior Cerebellar Artery)

### Origin & Course

**Origin Patterns**:
- 72% - Single trunk from basilar artery
- 26% - Duplicate AICA (two separate origins)
- 2% - Triplicate AICA (three origins)

**Location**: Lower to middle third of basilar artery

**Course**: Through cerebellopontine angle → Intimate relationship with CN VII/VIII → Petrosal cerebellar surface

---

### AICA Four-Segment Classification

| Segment | Anatomical Boundaries | Key Structures | Surgical Significance |
|---------|----------------------|----------------|----------------------|
| **1. Anterior Pontine** | Origin → CN VI | Pontine perforators | First perforators encountered |
| **2. Lateral Pontine** | CN VI → CN VII/VIII complex | Labyrinthine artery origin (77% from this region) | Most critical for CPA surgery |
| **3. Flocculopeduncular** | CN VII/VIII → Cerebellopontine fissure | Rostral/caudal bifurcation | Defines depth of dissection |
| **4. Cortical** | Beyond fissure | Terminal cerebellar branches | Can be sacrificed if needed (collaterals present) |

---

### AICA Nerve-Related Classification (Alternative)

| Segment | Definition | Frequency | Key Point |
|---------|------------|-----------|-----------|
| **Premeatal** | Basilar artery → CN VII/VIII | 88% solitary, 12% double | First segment encountered in retrosigmoid approach; 77% give labyrinthine artery |
| **Meatal** | Adjacent to internal auditory meatus | 82% one segment, 18% two | **50% reach porus or enter IAC** - at risk during IAC drilling |
| **Postmeatal** | Beyond meatus → Cerebellum | Variable | Subarcuate artery origin (42% from this segment) |

---

### AICA Branches

#### 1. Labyrinthine (Internal Auditory) Artery
- **Most consistent AICA branch** - supplies inner ear (cochlea and vestibular apparatus)
- **Quantity**: 30% have 1, 54% have 2, 14% have 3, 2% have 4
- **Origin**: 77% from premeatal, 21% from meatal, 2% from postmeatal segment
- **Clinical**: Occlusion → sudden profound hearing loss + vestibular symptoms
- **Surgical**: **Must be preserved** - no collateral circulation to inner ear

#### 2. Recurrent Perforating Arteries
- **Frequency**: Present in 82% of CPAs (74% have 1, 6% have 2, 2% have 3)
- **Course**: Origin toward meatus → recurrent course medially
- **Supply**: Middle cerebellar peduncle, pons (trigeminal entry zone), superolateral medulla, choroid plexus
- **Surgical**: **End-arteries - cannot be sacrificed** without brainstem infarction

#### 3. Subarcuate Artery
- **Frequency**: Present in 72% of CPAs (always single when present)
- **Origin**: 26% premeatal, 4% meatal, 42% postmeatal segment
- **Supply**: Petrous bone around semicircular canals
- **Clinical**: Potential route for mastoid infection → meningitis/superior petrosal sinus thrombosis

---

### AICA Bifurcation Patterns

**Typical Pattern**: Bifurcation into **rostral** and **caudal** trunks

- **Timing**: 2/3 bifurcate before crossing nerves, 1/3 after
- **Rostral trunk**: Above flocculus → Superior petrosal surface → Anastomoses with SCA
- **Caudal trunk**: Below flocculus → Inferior petrosal surface, lateral recess → Anastomoses with PICA

---

### AICA Occlusion Syndrome (Lateral Pontine Syndrome)

**Clinical Manifestations**:
- **CN VII**: Facial paralysis (ipsilateral)
- **CN VIII**: Deafness, vertigo, nystagmus (ipsilateral)
- **Trigeminal**: Facial pain/temperature loss, corneal hypesthesia (ipsilateral)
- **Autonomic**: Horner's syndrome (ipsilateral)
- **Cerebellar**: Ataxia, asynergia (ipsilateral)
- **Spinothalamic**: Incomplete contralateral body pain/temperature loss

**Spared**: Corticospinal tract, medial lemniscus (supplied by midline vertebrobasilar perforators)

---

## PICA (Posteroinferior Cerebellar Artery)

### Origin & Prevalence

**Origin from Vertebral Artery**:
- **Range**: 14mm below to 26mm above foramen magnum
- **Average**: 8.6mm above foramen magnum
- **Distance from VA-BA junction**: Average 16.9mm below

**Diameter at Origin**:
- Range: 0.5-3.4mm
- Average: 2.0mm

**Prevalence**:
- **84%** of hemispheres have PICA
- **16%** absent or hypoplastic
- Territory compensated by AICA or contralateral PICA when absent

---

### PICA Five-Segment Classification

| Segment | Anatomical Boundaries | Perforators (avg) | Key Structures | Surgical Importance |
|---------|----------------------|-------------------|----------------|---------------------|
| **1. Anterior Medullary** | Origin → Olive prominence | 1.0 | Anterior olive, CN XII rootlets | May be absent if low PICA origin |
| **2. Lateral Medullary** | Olive → CN IX/X/XI rootlets | 1.8 | **Lateral medulla** (PICA's "home territory") | Wallenberg syndrome if occluded |
| **3. Tonsillomedullary** | CN IX/X/XI → Mid-tonsil medial surface | **3.3** | Caudal (infratonsillar) loop | Most perforators; loop may dip below foramen magnum |
| **4. Telovelotonsillar** | Mid-tonsil → Suboccipital surface exit | Variable | Cranial (supratonsillar) loop, choroid plexus | Critical for telovelar approach |
| **5. Cortical** | Fissure exit → Terminal branches | 0 | Suboccipital cerebellar surface | Terminal distribution |

**Total Average Perforators**: 6.1 per hemisphere (range: 1-13)

**Key Point**: Tonsillomedullary segment is the most productive source of brainstem perforators

---

### PICA Loops Around the Tonsil

#### Caudal (Infratonsillar) Loop
- **Location**: At lower pole of cerebellar tonsil
- **Position relative to tonsil tip**: 55% superior, 19% inferior, 26% at same level
- **Position relative to foramen magnum**: 74% superior, 8% inferior, 2% at level
- **Range**: 7mm below to 18mm above foramen magnum (average 6.9mm above)
- **Clinical**: Important for far-lateral and foramen magnum approaches

#### Cranial (Supratonsillar) Loop
- **Location**: Between cerebellar tonsil (below) and tela choroidea/inferior medullary velum (above)
- **Apex**: Usually over central inferior medullary velum, inferior to fastigium
- **Branches**: Gives choroidal branches to fourth ventricle choroid plexus
- **Clinical**: **Defines lateral boundary in telovelar approach to fourth ventricle**

---

### PICA Bifurcation Patterns

**Standard Pattern**: Bifurcation into **medial** (smaller) and **lateral** (larger) trunks

- **Medial trunk**: Vermis + adjacent hemisphere
- **Lateral trunk**: Most of hemispheric + tonsillar suboccipital surface
- **Bifurcation site**: Most commonly in telovelotonsillar fissure around rostral tonsil pole

**Four Bifurcation Patterns** (based on location relative to tonsil):
1. **Inferolateral**: Lateral trunk courses over posterior tonsil surface
2. **Inferomedial**: Lateral trunk posterior to tonsil, medial trunk anteromedial around tonsil
3. **Superomedial**: Lateral trunk over medial tonsil surface, medial trunk to vermis
4. **Superolateral**: Lateral trunk lateral to tonsil, medial trunk to vermis

---

### PICA Anatomical Variations

| Variation | Frequency | Compensation | Surgical Impact |
|-----------|-----------|--------------|-----------------|
| **Absent PICA** | 16% | AICA or contralateral PICA | Must know territory compensation preoperatively |
| **Hypoplastic PICA** | 5-16% | Dominant AICA | PICA-AICA "balance" concept |
| **Duplicate PICA** | Rare | Two trunks from VA | More vessels at risk |
| **Azygous (bihemispheric) PICA** | Very rare | Single midline vessel supplies both sides | Midline approaches high risk |
| **Extradural origin** | Rare | C1/C2 VA origin, penetrates dura at dorsolateral medulla | **Critical to recognize on preop imaging** |
| **Fenestration** | Rare | Dual origin → reconstitution to single trunk | - |

---

### PICA Occlusion Syndrome (Lateral Medullary / Wallenberg Syndrome)

**Classic Triad**:
1. **Ipsilateral findings**: Facial pain/temperature loss, Horner's syndrome, ataxia
2. **Contralateral finding**: Body pain/temperature loss
3. **Bilateral findings**: Dysphagia, hoarseness, vertigo, nystagmus

**Detailed Manifestations**:
- **Nucleus ambiguus (CN IX/X)**: Dysphagia, hoarseness, vocal cord paralysis (ipsilateral)
- **Spinal trigeminal tract/nucleus**: Facial pain/temperature loss (ipsilateral)
- **Spinothalamic tract**: Body pain/temperature loss (contralateral)
- **Descending sympathetic fibers**: Horner's syndrome (ipsilateral)
- **Vestibular nuclei**: Vertigo, nausea, vomiting, nystagmus
- **Inferior cerebellar peduncle**: Ipsilateral ataxia, asynergia

**Spared**: Pyramidal tract (medial medulla) - no hemiparesis; medial lemniscus - preserved touch/proprioception

## AICA-PICA Integration

### Vascular Territories & Anastomoses

**Anastomotic Patterns**:
- **AICA caudal trunk ↔ PICA**: At cerebellar cortex
- **AICA rostral trunk ↔ SCA**: At cerebellar cortex

**Collateral Circulation**:
- **Cortical level**: Robust anastomoses provide collateral flow
- **Perforating arteries**: **End-arteries - no collaterals** → Infarction if sacrificed

**PICA-AICA Balance**:
- Dominant AICA → Small or absent PICA
- Dominant PICA → Smaller AICA territory
- Territory boundaries highly variable between individuals

## Surgical Pearls

### AICA Surgery

| Principle | Rationale |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Identify AICA early** | First landmark after dural opening in retrosigmoid CPA approach |
| **Check for meatal loop before IAC drilling** | 50% reach porus or enter canal - at risk for injury |
| **Preserve labyrinthine artery** | No collateral circulation to inner ear - injury = hearing loss |
| **Work in arachnoid plane** | Protects perforators and allows vessel mobilization |
| **Never sacrifice perforators** | End-arteries - sacrifice causes brainstem infarction |
| **Expect duplication (26%)** | Two AICAs on one side - must preserve both |

### PICA Surgery

| Principle | Rationale |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Mandatory preoperative angiography/MRA/CTA** | Extreme anatomical variability - cannot use textbook landmarks |
| **Identify caudal loop position** | May dip below foramen magnum (8%) - encountered during C1 arch removal |
| **PICA defines telovelar corridor** | Cranial loop is lateral boundary for fourth ventricle access |
| **Preserve perforators from tonsillomedullary segment** | Average 3.3 perforators - highest density of any segment |
| **Check for PICA absence (16%)** | Must know if AICA or contralateral PICA compensates |
| **Recognize extradural origin** | Rare but critical - injured during bone removal if not recognized preop |

### Universal Principles

1. **Arachnoid plane dissection**: Protects perforators and allows vessel mobilization without pia injury
2. **No bipolar on stretched vessels**: Relax brain first (CSF drainage, gentle retraction release)
3. **Cortical branches can be sacrificed if needed**: Collaterals present at cortical level
4. **Perforators are sacred**: End-arteries - sacrifice = infarction
5. **Know your patient's anatomy**: Preoperative imaging is not optional - variability is too high

## Approach-Specific Anatomy

### Retrosigmoid/CPA Approach
**Key Vessels**: AICA (primary), PICA caudal trunk (if low)
**First Landmark**: AICA premeatal segment after dural opening
**Critical Structures**: Labyrinthine artery, recurrent perforators, meatal loop
**Preserve**: CN VII/VIII, labyrinthine artery, arachnoid plane around perforators

### Telovelar (Fourth Ventricle) Approach
**Key Vessels**: PICA cranial loop (bilateral)
**Anatomical Corridor**: Between tonsils, inferior to cranial loop
**Lateral Boundary**: PICA defining safe working space
**Critical Structures**: Choroidal branches from telovelotonsillar segment, inferior medullary velum
**Variation Impact**: Early PICA bifurcation → more vessels in field

### Far-Lateral/Foramen Magnum Approach
**Key Vessels**: PICA caudal loop, vertebral artery
**Critical Assessment**: Does caudal loop dip below foramen magnum? (8% yes)
**Approach Steps**: Control VA → Identify PICA origin → Mobilize caudal loop → Access lateral medulla
**Extradural Origin**: Must recognize on preop imaging (rare but critical)

### Presigmoid/Transpetrosal Approach
**Key Vessels**: AICA segments, SCA
**Anatomical Exposure**: AICA segments from basilar origin to cortical distribution
**IAC Drilling**: Identify and preserve meatal loop before bone removal
**Expanded Exposure**: Allows proximal control for AICA aneurysms

## Summary Tables

### AICA vs PICA Comparison

| Feature | AICA | PICA |
|---------|------|------|
| **Origin** | Basilar artery (mid) | Vertebral artery |
| **Relationship** | CN VII/VIII | CN IX/X/XI/XII |
| **Primary Territory** | Anteroinferior cerebellum, middle peduncle, inner ear | Posteroinferior cerebellum, inferior peduncle, lateral medulla |
| **Segments** | 4 (anterior pontine, lateral pontine, flocculopeduncular, cortical) | 5 (anterior medullary, lateral medullary, tonsillomedullary, telovelotonsillar, cortical) |
| **Critical Loop** | Meatal loop (50% enter IAC) | Caudal + cranial loops around tonsil |
| **Most Important Branch** | Labyrinthine artery | Perforators to lateral medulla |
| **Variability** | Moderate (26% duplicate, loops variable) | **Highest of all cerebellar arteries** |
| **Absence Frequency** | Rare | 16% |
| **Occlusion Syndrome** | Lateral pontine (CN VII/VIII, vertigo, ataxia) | Lateral medullary / Wallenberg |
| **Surgical Approaches** | CPA, retrosigmoid, translabyrinthine | Far-lateral, telovelar, suboccipital |

### Segment-by-Segment Perforator Density

| Artery | Segment | Avg Perforators | Key Supply |
|--------|---------|----------------|-----------|
| **AICA** | Anterior pontine | Variable | Pons |
| **AICA** | Lateral pontine (nerve-related) | **Recurrent perforators (82% present)** | Middle cerebellar peduncle, trigeminal entry zone, superolateral medulla |
| **PICA** | Anterior medullary | 1.0 | Anterior medulla |
| **PICA** | Lateral medullary | 1.8 | **Lateral medulla** (primary territory) |
| **PICA** | Tonsillomedullary | **3.3** (highest density) | Posterior medulla, brainstem |
| **PICA** | Telovelotonsillar | Variable | Choroid plexus |
